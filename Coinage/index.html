Pre-Decimal Coinage

Introduction

Since the 15th of February 1971, the United Kingom has used a decimal currency. This means that the base subunit of a penny is worth 1/100 of the main unit of a pound.

However, before 'Decimalisation Day', the United Kingdom used a complicated system called £sd, which holds its origin in Roman currency.

£ - pound (from Latin lībra, meaning 'pound' (i.e. the unit of weight, in this case a pound of gold)

s - shilling (from Latin solidus, meaning 'solid')

d - penny (from Latin denarius, meaning 'ten of')

This guide will attempt to elucidate the most common denominations of the former British Pound. Not all of these will be contemporaneous, however the literature is shaky so the author has included as many denominations as possible.
