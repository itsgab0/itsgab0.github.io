Genuinely Nonsensical Idioms

What is an idiom?

An idiom is a figure of speech, that is, a word or phrase that is used figuratively to convey another meaning. In many cases, the basis of the idioms can be figured out, e.g.,

It's raining cats and dogs - It's raining heavily

The rain is so heavy that it no longer sounds like rain, it sounds almost like entire animals are falling from the sky

But this is not always the case, sometimes idioms show up having been uttered for centuries, and the context is all but lost. This is especially interesting when other languages are taken into account, as both lack of context and words that don't fully translate makes for some very weird idioms. The author has collected a few of their favourite examples. The original idiom is given at the top, the official translation/English equivalent is given on the left, and the literal translation is on the right.
