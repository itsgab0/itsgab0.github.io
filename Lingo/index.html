21st Century Lingo

What is 21st Century Lingo?

With the turn of the millennium comes a whole new generation of people with their own culture and way of speaking. In fact, as of right now there are two notable 'young' generations:

The Millennials (Generation Y) and The Zoomers (Generation Z)

The definitions for these generations are vague, however the consensus is that the term ‘Millennial’ roughly corresponds to those born from the early 1980s through to the late 1990s; the term ‘Generation Z’ roughly corresponds to those born from the late 1990s through to the early 2010s.

Both generations are notable for having grown up in a new age of technology, as such the use of modern media is facile. As generations before them, they have a distinct sense of humour and ways of communicating. A shared outlook on life along with the evolution of humour into irony and postirony along with new forms of absurdism and escapism are instrumental in the formation of new words and phrases.

Also present in this vocabulary is the marked influences from other forms of English, such as AAVE (African American Vernacular English), MLE (Multicultural London English), etc. Each of these are notable for being the representations of multiple ethnic/cultural backgrounds to produce a unified manner of speaking in certain communities.

Additionally, online use is characterised by frequent use of abbreviations, initialisms, and acronyms. These don't translate particularly well into spoken speech, and are typically replaced by their 'full forms'. Do not expect 'perfect' spelling nor grammar to be portrayed either, this guide is entirely descriptive and makes no attempt to suggest a 'correct' way of speaking. This guide also does not include any emojis, which are a whole different kettle of fish.

It should come as no surprise to anyone who has ever interacted with someone in the 21st century demographic, but no matter how clearly the use of any of the terms in this guide is understood by the reader, any attempt to recreate the usage will inevitably reveal oneself as an infiltrator due to the impossible levels of nuance packed into every single character. Additionally, an effort has been made to omit terms that, while popular today amongst the young generations, have seen great use in decades past - this is to maximise the '21st century feel' of the guide.

Within this guide, broad IPA transcriptions are given. These are from the perspective of a Received Pronunciation accent except in rare cases. For assistance, see here. For the most part, these are words that either already existed, or are adaptations of other words. No etymologies are given except for especially esoteric terms. This is partially due to the current lack of written documentation on certain terms.

This guide will inevitably contain lots of profanity, so if that is something the reader deems unacceptable, the author offers this advice:

1) Grow up.
