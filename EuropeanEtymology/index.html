Etymology of European Countries

Where do the Names of the European Countries come from?

Europe is a complex hodge-podge of a continent, with regards to official languages. Of the countries typically included in the bounds of Europe, 9 language families come out of it - not even all of them are Indo-European.

  • Afro-Asiatic - Semitic
  • Indo-European - Albanian, Baltic, Celtic, Germanic, Hellenic, Romance, Slavic
  • Uralic - Uralic

As such, we find no consistent naming pattern when it comes to the names of these countries, both natively and in English. The author has outlined the origins of all the native and English names of the European countries. Multiple entries at the same level of indentation implies there are multiple theories as to the origin; e.g. 'Malta' could be from the Phoenician 'mlt' or the Greek 'mélitos'. An indented origin implies a progression; e.g. 'Austria' comes from the Old High German 'Ostarrîchi' which in turn comes from the Latin 'marchia orientalis'.
