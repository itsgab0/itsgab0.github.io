British Monarchs

Who are the British Monarchs?

The British Monarchs have ruled over varying territories over the years thanks to the British Empire, however the sights were not always set on global domination. The topic of which countries should be under the rule of the King/Queen of England has always been slightly contentious.

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland - as it stands today - includes the following countries:

  • England
  • Scotland
  • Wales
  • Northern Ireland

* The English Bill of Rights 1689 dictated that Mary II and her husband William of Orange would co-rule the nation until one's death, whereupon the remaining diarch would assume complete rule. Mary died first, and William was left as sole ruler until his death.

† Cwichelm of Wessex is a monarch shrouded in some mystery. Sometimes he is labelled as a distinct King of Wessex, and other times he is conflated with the contemporaneous Cynegils as either the same person, or as the latter's son.
